Project C

For this project, I worked with three team members to design and develop a fully functional web application. The assignment was to create a matching website where users could create a profile, set their preferences, and get matched with others. Our team chose a unique approach: BandBuddy, an app that connects musicians.

BandBuddy solves a common problem in the music industry: finding the right musical partners nearby. Whether you're looking for a drummer for your band, a guitarist to form a duo, or just someone to jam with, BandBuddy makes it happen.

Development Progress

Initial Design

In the initial phase, I worked closely with another front-end developer to research various matching apps. We analyzed their strengths and weaknesses, identifying key bad, good, and best practices to apply to our design.

Based on our findings, we created moodboards and a styleboard to define BandBuddy’s visual identity. We also developed a user persona representing our target audience, which guided our design choices.

To plan the app's structure, we created a wireflow mapping out the necessary pages and user flows. With these insights, we created initial sketches, experimenting with layouts, color combinations, and interactive elements, while ensuring the app supported dark mode and multiple languages.

Development

In the development phase, I was responsible for coding the last five registration pages, as well as the profile, settings and chat pages. In the root of the project, we set up reusable components for colors and text.

Additionally, I worked with the other front-end developer to design various states, where I was responsible for the 404 and completed states.

I was also in charge of setting up and maintaining the styleboard, ensuring visual consistency across the app. Additionally, I kept the wireflow updated to visualize the app's structure and user flow.

Results

Due to time constraints, we were unable to implement certain features, such as the chat functionality and some states like 404 and completed. Additionally, we couldn't fully code dark mode, but we did create a sketch to visualize how it would look. Despite these limitations, we focused on delivering a working app with the essential features.

BandBuddy now allows musicians to create profiles, set preferences, and find other musicians nearby. The "For You" page lets users swipe through profiles and connect with others. You can also update your profile, including adding photos and videos, and these changes are directly saved to the database. Users have the option to delete their profiles as well. The design is clean, simple, and consistent, providing a smooth user experience.

Reflections

This project taught me a lot about working within time constraints and prioritizing features. Despite some features being left out, the app is still functional and meets its main goals. It was a valuable experience in full-stack development, from front-end design to back-end functionality. In the process, I also gained a solid understanding of how to create and manipulate a database, improving my coding skills overall.

We worked with the assumption that we already knew front-end development, so there was no direct guidance in the design process. However, I quickly found strategies that worked, like conducting competitor research, creating a persona, moodboard/styleboard and wireflow. These steps helped streamline the design and made the project smoother. In future projects, I’ll focus more on time management to ensure all features are fully implemented and polished.
