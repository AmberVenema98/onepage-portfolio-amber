During my short 10 week internship at S.P. Abonneeservice, I had the opportunity to work on improving the online forms, the subscription overview page, and the contact page for two publishers: OOR and NIW. The goal of the project was to simplify and accelerate the subscription process for users while creating a more user-friendly and intuitive experience.

My tasks included modifying forms in Quartz, designing updated web pages in Photoshop, and implementing responsive prototypes using Bootstrap. Throughout the internship, I collaborated closely with my supervisor and the publishers to ensure that the designs met their expectations.

Development Progress

Initial Design

The initial design phase focused on exploring ways to streamline the user experience. For the forms, I analyzed the existing structure to identify areas where steps could be reduced or unnecessary fields removed. For the subscription overview page, I aimed to create a clear and organized layout that simplified the process of selecting an appropriate subscription. On the contact page, I added visual elements like icons to improve readability and navigation. I created wireframes and design mockups in Photoshop during this stage, drawing inspiration from successful elements in previous S.P. Abonneeservice projects.

Development

After finalizing the initial designs, I began the development phase. Using Quartz, I modified the online forms to make them more compact and efficient. For the subscription overview and contact pages, I used Photoshop to refine the visual elements and Bootstrap to implement responsive, functional prototypes. This allowed the pages to be accessible on a variety of devices. Testing was a key part of this phase, as I created multiple versions of the designs and adjusted them based on feedback from my supervisor and the publishers. These prototypes were aimed at reducing user friction while maintaining a clean and professional appearance.

Results

The final deliverables included a set of user-friendly forms, a redesigned subscription overview page, and an improved contact page. The forms were simplified, requiring fewer steps for users to complete. The subscription overview page provided a clearer and more structured way for users to explore and select subscriptions, while the contact page was redesigned to make key information more accessible with the help of icons and visual hierarchy. Positive feedback from both my supervisor and the publishers confirmed that the designs successfully met their expectations and improved the user experience.

Reflections

This internship was an invaluable learning experience that allowed me to apply my skills in a real-world setting. By working closely with clients and collecting feedback throughout the process, I significantly improved my communication and collaboration skills. I also enhanced my ability to explain and justify design decisions, which was essential when presenting to the publishers. Furthermore, I developed a stronger technical foundation in Quartz, Photoshop, and Bootstrap, which boosted my confidence in tackling future projects.

Overall, this internship provided me with a deeper understanding of user-centered design and how to implement it in a professional context. It inspired me to continue exploring ways to create intuitive and effective digital experiences.
